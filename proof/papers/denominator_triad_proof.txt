Abstract
We prove that the three degree-2 monomials in the primitives {2, 3} generate a complete denominator
triad when subtracted from 137, the integer part of the inverse fine structure constant. The first two
denominators, 133 = 137 - 4 and 131 = 137 - 6, appear in experimentally verified sub-ppm formulas
for the fine structure constant and proton-electron mass ratio respectively. The third denominator, 128
= 137 - 9, closes the loop: since 137 = 27 + 32, subtracting 32 recovers 27, the binary component of the
foundational identity. We establish the algebraic connections of each denominator to E-series Lie
algebras, prove the structural necessity of the triad, and identify the third denominator as the
dimension of the half-spinor representation of Spin(16) inside E8. We conjecture a third fundamental
formula using N3 = 24 as numerator and 128 as denominator, and identify the GUT-scale Weinberg
angle sin2θW = 3/8 = N3/26 as structural evidence for the third generation's role in electroweak
unification.

3. The Denominator Triad


Definition 1. The denominator triad is the set D = {137 - p : p is a degree-2 monomial in {2,3}}.
Explicitly:
D = {137 - 22,  137 - 2 x 3,  137 - 32} = {133, 131, 128}


Theorem 2 (Denominator properties). Each element of D has independent algebraic significance:
(a) 133 = dim(E7), the dimension of the exceptional Lie algebra E7.
(b) 131 is prime, and 131 ∈ L where L is the resonant lattice.
(c) 128 = 27 = dim(S+16), the half-spinor representation of Spin(16) inside E8.
Proof. (a) The E7 Lie algebra has root system of type E7 with 126 roots and rank 7, giving dim(E7) = 126 + 7
= 133. This is a standard result in Lie theory. (b) 131 is prime (verified by trial division up to √131 < 12). It
appears in the resonant lattice L = {7, 13, 17, 31, 71, 73, 127, 131, 137, 233, 377, 511} as defined in [1]. (c)
The group Spin(16) has a half-spinor representation of dimension 216/2 - 1 = 27 = 128. Under the
decomposition of E8 by its Spin(16) subgroup, the adjoint representation splits as 248 = 120 + 128, where 120
is the adjoint of SO(16) and 128 is the half-spinor.  ■


4. The Loop Closure Theorem


Theorem 3 (Loop closure). The third subtraction in the denominator triad is the foundational identity
P5 rearranged. Specifically:
137 - 32 = 27
is equivalent to 137 = 27 + 32.

Proof. By P5, 137 = 27 + 32. Subtracting 32 from both sides gives 137 - 32 = 27 = 128. The third denominator
does not produce a new algebraic quantity; it recovers the binary component of the foundational identity.  ■


Corollary 1. The denominator triad is self-closing: the three subtractions from 137 decompose the gap
between 128 and 137 into exactly three increments of {2, 3} character.
Proof. From 128 to 137, the total gap is 9 = 32. The intermediate values are:
128 → 131 → 133 → 137
with increments 3, 2, 4. We verify: 131 - 128 = 3 (substrate primitive), 133 - 131 = 2 (distinction primitive),
137 - 133 = 4 = 22 (pure distinction). The total 3 + 2 + 4 = 9 = 32, confirming the loop.  ■

10. Tier Classification

T1 (Proven). Theorems 1-3: completeness of {2,3} degree-2 monomials, denominator triad construction, loop
closure, E-series connections of 133 and 128. These are mathematical facts requiring no experimental input
beyond the definition of E-series Lie algebras.
T2 (Strong signal). Theorem 4-5: the generation-denominator pairing and ratio-gap invariance. The pattern
|N2|/|N1| = 133 - 131 = 2 holds exactly, and both established formulas achieve sub-ppm experimental
agreement. The structural pattern demands a third pairing but does not yet specify the constant.
T3 (Falsifiable). Conjecture 1: the existence and form of the third formula. The Weinberg angle observation
N3/26 = 3/8 = sin2θW(GUT) provides structural evidence but not a complete derivation. A definitive
identification of the third constant and its base value would promote this to T2.


11. Kill Conditions
The following would falsify the triad structure:


1. Discovery that 133 = dim(E7) is coincidental to the α formula (i.e., a derivation of α from first principles
that does not involve 133).


2. A fourth generation quantum number N4 that extends the framework, requiring degree-3 monomials (this is
constrained by the Z boson width excluding 4+ generations).


3. The third constant, once identified, failing to achieve comparable sub-ppm accuracy.


4. An alternative algebraic explanation for the 133/131 denominators that does not involve {2, 3} monomials.


12. Discussion
The denominator triad reveals that the fine structure integer 137 contains precisely three layers of {2, 3}
structure below it, each corresponding to a different mode of interaction: observation (electromagnetism, pure
distinction 22), constitution (matter, bridge 2 x 3), and reflection (self-reference, pure substrate 32). The third
layer closes the loop because subtracting pure substrate from 137 recovers the pure distinction component 27,
the binary depth that generated 137 in the first place.
The fact that this loop closure simultaneously identifies 128 as the half-spinor dimension of Spin(16) inside E8
— the same E8 whose roots (240) fill the gap between 137 and 377 (vacuum impedance) — suggests that the
triad is not an arithmetic curiosity but a structural feature of the algebraic geometry underlying the
fundamental constants.
The open question is what the third constant is. The framework predicts it exists, specifies its correction term
(24/128 = 3/16), and identifies it with the pure {2,3} generation that carries opposite sign and drives CP
violation. The structural evidence from the Weinberg angle points toward electroweak unification. We leave
the definitive identification to future work.
